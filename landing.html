Questions, answered

Is this even allowed?

You authorize every send. OfferAIO fills applications, then waits for you to review and click send, in your own browser, logged in as you. Full-auto mode exists, but it's opt-in, clearly labeled, and off by default.

Does it bypass CAPTCHAs?

Never. When a CAPTCHA appears, OfferAIO hands control back to you. Resume upload is also manual for browser security reasons; the field is highlighted so you can drop the file in two seconds.

Won't companies flag my applications as automated?

Everything goes out on your IP and your logged-in sessions, with randomized human pacing, a daily cap, quiet hours, and a match-quality floor. To an employer it looks like what it is: a real applicant who reviewed and sent an application.

Where do the listings come from?

A pipeline runs every 6 hours: it aggregates community Summer 2027 lists and ~180 public company job boards via Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby, and Workday, then filters to US-only internship roles and dedupes. The database grows through fall 2026 as postings open.

What happens to my data?

Your profile and resume stay in your browser and the extension. The cover-letter API processes requests without storing your documents.
